2015 Annual Report

In compiling this year’s annual report, we couldn’t help but marvel at the scope of the Wisconsin Energy Institute’s reach in 2014. This year, perhaps more than any other, we made good on our goal of becoming international leaders in clean energy research and outreach. Whether educating students on energy issues, studying public policy, engaging with industry, or developing the technologies that will transform the energy market, we put more weight than ever into solving the world’s energy problems.

WEI’s mission to foster collaborative energy projects was taken up by hundreds of researchers at the University of Wisconsin–Madison and its partnering institutions. The diverse group of researchers at the Great Lakes Bioenergy Research Center (GLBRC), for example, strengthened their status as internationally recognized experts in bioenergy, publishing two research papers in Nature and two in Science, and filing their 100th patent application.

Forward in Energy

  1. Letter from the Director

    The Wisconsin Energy Institute (WEI) at the University of Wisconsin-Madison received Project of the Year and Best Green-Built honors for its dedication to sustainability in design, construction and functionality at this year's Commercial Design Awards.

  2. 2015 Timeline

    Connecting with industry and government. Organizing events, student competitions, and educational institutes. Welcoming groups from all over the world.

World-Class Energy Research

  1. Boosting Battery Technology

    A novel X-ray imaging technique could improve the energy storage of everything from portable electronics to electric microgrids.

  2. Cutting Carbon from U.S. Highways

    Modeling experts provided a blueprint for a 50% reduction in gasoline use and an 80% reduction in carbon emissions.

  3. Creating the Solar Textile

    Together a chemistry professor and a textile expert are developing fabric capable of harvesting energy from the sun.

  4. Engineering for Energy Savings

    A new method that makes softwoods easier to process could improve the economics of the paper and biofuels industries and reduce their environmental impact.

  5. Developing a Sustainable Biofuel Pipeline

    A six-year study showed that perennial cropping systems produce enough biomass to make them competitive with corn.

  6. Advancing Agriculture

    The newly discovered chemical compound poacic acid could become a fungicide in both sustainable and conventional farming.

Far-Reaching Education and Outreach Efforts

  1. Training the Trainers

    Teachers participating in the Energy Institute for Educators explored ways of bringing new energy research into their science, technology, engineering, and math classrooms.

  2. Connecting to Schools

    Marin Dobson, a high school teacher in Fort Atkinson, Wis., used his experiences working with WEI to help create a new scientific curriculum for his school.

  3. Inspiring Students to Innovate

    Two rounds of the Wisconsin Energy and Sustainability Challenge rewarded undergraduate students for developing novel energy technologies and approaches to global stewardship.

  4. Preparing Students to Lead

    Recent graduate Evan Petkov is one of nearly 100 Badgers to complete the Undergraduate Certificate of Engineering and Sustainability co-run by WEI and the College of Engineering.

  5. Generating Discussion Across Sectors

    WEI’s third annual Energy Summit drew 285 attendees from industry, government, and regulatory agencies to discuss “Air & Energy: The Path Ahead for U.S. States.”

High Impact Public Policy and Industry Engagement

  1. Strengthening Wisconsin’s Industries

    WEI and M-WERC, a Milwaukee-based consortium of researchers and industry representatives, continue to usher clean energy technologies out of the lab and into the marketplace.

  2. Using New Technologies in Wisconsin

    Researchers are using state-of-the-art microgrid technology to help revitalize Milwaukee’s industry.

  3. Studying Geothermal Systems in Rural Wisconsin

    Eleanor Bloom, a UW–Madison graduate student in geological engineering, thinks geothermal systems like the one she studies in Grand Marsh, Wis. could someday heat and cool rural Wisconsin homes.

  4. Rethinking the Electrical Grid

    Our new “Power Points” blog focused on the efforts of utilities, policymakers, and innovators to provide affordable, renewable, and reliable energy to consumers.
